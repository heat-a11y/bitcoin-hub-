⏱ DCA + Dip Buy

Recurring buys every week + extra buys when price drops 10%+ from your entry.

  • Set weekly recurring buy (e.g. $50)
  • Set alerts for -10%, -20%, -30% drops
  • Buy 2x, 3x, 5x your weekly on dips
  • Transfer to cold storage monthly
  • Never sell during bear markets

🔍 Liquidity Sweep

Place limit orders at known liquidity zones where price tends to bounce.

  • Identify order book bid clusters
  • Place limit orders below cluster
  • Set take-profit at next resistance
  • Repeat as liquidity pools shift
  • Use exchange order books + on-chain data

Rotate between BTC, ecosystem projects, and stablecoins based on cycle indicators.

Myths That Need to Die

Common misconceptions about Bitcoin — debunked.

▶ ❌  "Only used by criminals"
Cash is used by criminals too. Blockchains are actually more traceable than cash. Legitimate users, companies, and even countries use Bitcoin every day. The public ledger means every transaction is permanently recorded.
▶ ❌  "It's a bubble that will go to zero"
People have called it a bubble every year since 2011. It's still here. Volatility is real, but Bitcoin has become a trillion-dollar asset class adopted by institutions, corporations, and sovereign wealth funds worldwide.
▶ ❌  "Bad for the environment"
Over 50% of mining uses renewable energy. Bitcoin mining actually incentivizes green energy development by monetizing stranded energy and methane capture that would otherwise be wasted or flared.
▶ ❌  "I need to buy a whole Bitcoin"
Bitcoin is divisible to 8 decimal places. You can buy $10 worth. The smallest unit is a satoshi = 0.00000001 BTC. You don't need to be rich to start.
▶ ❌  "It's too late"
Less than 5% of the world's population owns Bitcoin. Adoption is still incredibly early. The best time to learn was 10 years ago. The second best time is right now.
▶ ❌  "Bitcoin is too volatile to be money"
Volatility decreases over time as market cap grows. Bitcoin's 4-year cycle volatility is declining. It's a store of value first, medium of exchange second — like digital gold with payment rails.

📦 Understanding UTXOs: The Building Blocks of Bitcoin Transactions

Every Bitcoin transaction consumes and creates Unspent Transaction Outputs (UTXOs). Think of UTXOs like physical coins — you can't spend half a coin; you spend the whole thing and get change back.

Key Insight: Your Bitcoin wallet doesn't contain a "balance" — it contains a collection of UTXOs that sum to your total.

Why UTXOs Matter

  • Privacy: UTXOs can be linked to you if you combine them carelessly
  • Fees: More UTXOs = larger transaction = higher fees
  • Coin Control: Choose which UTXOs to spend for better privacy
  • Dust: Tiny UTXOs may cost more to spend than they're worth

Best Practices

  • Use coin control features in wallets like Sparrow or Electrum
  • Consolidate UTXOs when fees are low
  • Avoid reusing addresses
  • Label your UTXOs to track their origin

🔒 The Complete Guide to Taproot: Bitcoin's Biggest Privacy Upgrade

Activated in November 2021, Taproot (BIP 340–342) is Bitcoin's most significant upgrade since SegWit. It introduces Schnorr signatures, MAST, and a new scripting paradigm.

Key Insight: Taproot makes complex transactions look like simple ones on the blockchain, dramatically improving privacy.

What Taproot Changes

  • Schnorr Signatures (BIP 340): Faster, smaller, and enable signature aggregation
  • Taproot (BIP 341): MAST allows conditional spending without revealing unused conditions
  • Tapscript (BIP 342): Upgraded scripting language with Schnorr support

Real-World Impact

  • Multisig transactions look identical to single-sig
  • Lightning Network channels benefit from reduced transaction size
  • Enables advanced smart contracts with better privacy

🪙 Mastering Coin Control: Why Every Bitcoiner Needs It

When you send Bitcoin, your wallet automatically selects which UTXOs to spend. This coin selection can leak your privacy if you're not careful.

Key Insight: Automatic coin selection often combines UTXOs from different sources, linking them on the blockchain forever.

Coin Control Best Practices

  • Use wallets with manual coin selection (Sparrow, Electrum, Blue Wallet)
  • Keep UTXOs from different sources separate
  • Use "coin control" features to pick specific UTXOs
  • Consider "consolidation transactions" during low fee periods
  • Label every UTXO with its origin

Tools that Support Coin Control

  • Sparrow Wallet — Best-in-class coin control UI
  • Electrum — Classic, powerful, supports labels
  • Blue Wallet — Mobile-friendly with basic coin control
  • Bitcoin Core — Full node with complete UTXO management
